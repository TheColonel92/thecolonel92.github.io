University Bachelors of Technology Data Science
The University Bachelor of Technology (B.U.T.) in Statistics and Business Intelligence (SD) is a three-year program designed to train professionals capable of assisting decision-making through data management, statistical analysis, and programming. Graduates are skilled in data collection, ensuring quality, security, and storage in structured databases, and performing statistical analyses to create decision-making tools.

Study Tracks:
1. Statistical Exploration and Modelling
This track trains professionals in data collection, processing, and statistical analysis. Students learn to identify and apply appropriate statistical tools to solve specific problems and present results in the form of reports, tables, and graphs.
Career prospects: Statistical studies manager, data analyst, data scientist, statistician, marketing research manager.
2. Visualization and Design of Decision-Making Tools
This track focuses on the implementation of decision-making solutions, including automating various steps of the decision process. Students gain expertise in managing data connections, transformation (ETL), and business calculations, as well as producing data visualizations through dashboards and reporting tools for non-technical end-users.
Career prospects: Business intelligence (BI) developer, data manager, project manager, big data developer.

Core Skills:
The program develops four core competencies:
  • Data processing for decision-making
  • Statistical data analysis
  • Professional data visualization and reporting
  • Specialized skills depending on the chosen track:
    • Statistical Exploration and Modelling: Developing statistical models
    • Visualization and Design of Decision-Making Tools: Creating decision-support tools


Admission Requirements:
The program is open to high school graduates from general or technological backgrounds, as well as university students seeking to change their academic path. Admissions are based on academic records, and some departments may conduct interviews. The program can also be pursued through lifelong learning or apprenticeship contracts.
